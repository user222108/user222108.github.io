HONORARY CONSUL OF THAILAND IN ESTONIA

On 11 May 2015, a formal ceremony was held in Tallinn to present the exequatur to Mr. Enn Pant, officially authorizing him to assume the role of Honorary Consul of the Kingdom of Thailand in Estonia for the first time.

Following a brief intermission in his consular service, Mr. Pant resumed his official duties in 2025, reaffirming his commitment to representing Thailand’s interests in Estonia.

His appointments underscore the Kingdom of Thailand’s enduring dedication to fostering strong diplomatic, economic, and cultural relations with the Republic of Estonia.

Brief overview of the career of Enn Pant

Enn Pant (born 1965) has been Chairman of the Supervisory Board of AS Tallink Grupp since 2015. He holds positions in AS Tallink Grupp consolidation group’s subsidiaries’ managerial bodies. From 1996 to 2015 he held the position of Chairman of the Management Board and CEO of AS Tallink Grupp. He also holds the positions of Chairman of the Supervisory Board at AS Infortar and President of the Estonian Tennis Federation since 2013. He was the Chancellor of the Ministry of Finance of Estonia from 1992 to 1996. Enn Pant graduated from the Faculty of Economics, Tartu University, Estonia, in 1990.

ESTONIA & THAILAND: HISTORICAL RELATIONSHIP

Thailand, officially the Kingdom of Thailand, formerly known as Siam. Siam was first mentioned in Estonian writings in professor M. von Engelhardt’s short geographical piece in 1824. In 1849, Thailand (then known as Siam) and its capital Bangkok were mentioned in Berend Gildermann’s textbook. The first reports about Thailand (Siam) appeared in “Perno Postimees” in 1857.

Siam, as a founding member of the League of Nations, recognized the Republic of Estonia when it joined that organization on 22 October 1921. The Kingdom of Thailand re-recognized the Republic of Estonia on 9 September 1991.

Diplomatic relations between the two states were re-established on 27 April 1992.

VISA AND CONSULAR SECTION

Please note that Estonia does not currently host a full-service Thai consulate. For any Thai visa applications, passport renewals, or consular matters, the nearest official point of contact is the Royal Thai Embassy in Helsinki, Finland: helsinki.thaiembassy.org.

For official updates, consular announcements, or public notices, please consult the verified Facebook page: Royal Thai Embassy, Helsinki (Facebook).
